Getting There

Travel & Hotels

We are so excited to celebrate with you! Since many of our guests will be traveling internationally, here are the easiest ways to get to Puebla.

✈️ Option 1: Fly Directly to Puebla (PBC)

You can fly directly into Hermanos Serdán International Airport (PBC) in Puebla. Once you arrive, you can easily take an Uber to your hotel.

Nonstop from Newark (EWR)

There is a nonstop flight from Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR) to Puebla (PBC) operated by Volaris. This is a new route with flights departing Mondays, Wednesdays, Fridays, and Sundays.

From JFK via Cancun

A comfortable option is flying JFK → Cancun → Puebla (PBC). Cancun's airport is easy to navigate and this tends to be a smooth connection.

From JFK via Houston

You can also fly JFK → Houston → Puebla (PBC).

⚠️ Please be aware that flights from Houston to Puebla are sometimes delayed or rescheduled.

✈️ Option 2: Fly to Mexico City (CDMX)

Another very common option is flying into Mexico City International Airport (AICM). No matter where you're departing from, it's usually easy to find frequent flights here. Airlines that fly directly from JFK to Mexico City include Delta, Aeroméxico, VivaAerobus, and American Airlines.

Bus from Mexico City to Puebla

Once you arrive at AICM, we recommend taking a 2.5-hour bus ride to Puebla. Buses depart directly from Terminal 1 and Terminal 2 and arrive at Terminal Paseo Destino in Puebla. The two main bus companies are ADO and Estrella Roja, with multiple departures every hour. Tickets typically cost around $25 USD.

Check schedules and buy tickets: www.ado.com.mx  ·  www.estrellaroja.com.mx
We strongly recommend taking the bus instead of renting a car. It is comfortable, safe, and the easiest way to reach Puebla. If you decide to rent a car, we recommend major international companies such as Hertz, Enterprise, or similar providers.

Our wedding will take place at the Museo San Pedro de Arte, located in Puebla's beautiful Centro Histórico. We recommend staying in or near the historic center, where you'll be close to restaurants, museums, and many of the city's main attractions. In addition to these options, we encourage guests to explore Airbnb, especially if you're traveling with a group or looking for a longer stay.

Things to Do

Explore Puebla

Puebla is one of Mexico's most beautiful and culturally rich cities — from ancient pyramids to colonial baroque churches, world-class cuisine, and incredible day trips. We hope you take some extra time to explore! For a full guide to the city, visit visitpuebla.mx.

Day Trips & Excursions

Wildlife · 30 min from Puebla

Africam Safari

One of the most unique safari parks in Latin America. Drive through open habitats and come face to face with lions, giraffes, elephants, and hundreds of exotic species.

Archaeological Site · 20 min from Puebla

Pirámide de Cholula

The largest pyramid by volume in the world, hidden beneath a hill with a 16th-century Spanish church on top. Walk the tunnels and enjoy sweeping views of Popocatépetl.

Village · 45 min from Puebla

Val'Quirico

A charming Tuscan-inspired village in the mountains near Tlaxcala. Cobblestone streets, artisan boutiques, wine bars, and scenic mountain views.

Flower Town · 40 min from Puebla

Atlixco

Known as the City of Flowers, Atlixco is a charming colonial town famous for its gardens, flower festivals, gorgeous main plaza, and relaxed atmosphere — a wonderful half-day trip.

Historic Estate · 1 hr from Puebla

Ex-Hacienda de Chautla

A magnificent 19th-century hacienda nestled in the mountains of Puebla. Stunning Victorian-era architecture, beautiful gardens, a lake, and a charming chapel — like stepping into another era.

Museums · Centro Histórico

World-Class Museums

Don't miss the Museo Amparo (pre-Hispanic and colonial art), the Museo Internacional del Barroco (architecture by Toyo Ito), Museo de Arte Popular, and our own wedding venue — Museo San Pedro de Arte.

In the City

Historic Center · Walking Distance

Centro Histórico de Puebla

Puebla's UNESCO-listed historic center is one of the best-preserved colonial cities in the Americas. Stroll past Talavera-tiled facades, baroque churches, the Zócalo, and the Cathedral. Don't miss the Barrio de los Sapos for antiques and craft markets.

Landmark · Puebla City

La Estrella de Puebla

One of the tallest observation wheels in Latin America at 80 meters high. Ride the gondolas for breathtaking 360-degree views of Puebla, the surrounding volcanoes, and the city skyline.

History & Views · Puebla

Los Fuertes de Puebla

The historic forts of Loreto and Guadalupe, where the famous Battle of Puebla took place on May 5, 1862. Panoramic views of the city, museums, and the true story behind Cinco de Mayo.

Food & Culture · Must Do

Mercado El Parián & Local Food

Puebla is the birthplace of mole poblano and chiles en nogada. Explore Mercado El Parián for Talavera crafts and local sweets. Try a cemita, a chalupa, or the famous mole at any of the city's celebrated restaurants.
